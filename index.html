    iCAN

    iCAN, the International College Application Network, is a blog dedicated to helping high school international students understand the American college application process. Since the college application process in the United States is extremely different than that of other countries, I have created and am working towards a series of blog posts that explain everything from when to take the SATs to how to approach the principal for a letter of recommendation. Having lived in India for the two most important years of high school, and going through the process of college applications without a college counsellor, I found that it might be helpful for other students to share the knowledge I garnered from my own experience.

    Duke Medical Center Intern

    I am a medical intern at the Duke Department of Speech Pathology, where I have garnered extensive experience conducting data analysis, data mining, and manuscript preparation alongside my PI, Dr. Harrison Jones. Currently, I am working on an independent research project regarding Respiratory Muscle Treatment (RMT) and device calibration. I am writing a paper of publishable quality in the hopes of contributing to the understanding of RMT devices, their calibration, and their importance in the clinical world with vulnerable patient populations.

    Gestational Diabetes Matlab Script

    I am working towards synthesizing a MatLab script to calculate average calorie intake pregnant mothers need for adequate fetal growth through each week in the gestational period. In addition to the calorific intake per day, the script will adjust the calorie input accordingly based on the blood glucose and insulin in the body to provide nutrition advising to mothers with gestational diabetes.
